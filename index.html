The Genesis Center

An all-expenses-paid co-learning residency dedicated to the accelerated advancement of the next generation of Bitcoin creators.

What we do

The Genesis Center is a self-directed, community-driven educational retreat for Bitcoin creators in New York City.

Immerse Yourself

Spend three months learning as much as you can. We provide all of the resources to surround yourself with the most interesting peers and the best opportunities that Bitcoin has to offer.

Build a Network

Share your days with the brightest minds from around the world. We provide industry leading experts as mentors and connect you with the people working on bleeding-edge projects in your area of interest.

Contribute

Help make Bitcoin better by building your own project or contributing to the open source projects of your choosing. This helps you establish your reputation and improves the ecosystem.

About

This is a three-month program hosted in New York City. Residents are expected to commit to coming into the Center every work day. We will provide monthly stipends as well as cover travel and lodging to ensure this opportunity is available to all candidates regardless of financial circumstance.

There are no instructors or daily lectures at the Genesis Center. The residents are responsible for their own education. Self-direction does not imply isolation or a lack of structure. Whether participants are working together or in parallel, they will be working towards the common goal of improving Bitcoin.


Who we are looking for

Ideal applicants are experienced coders, designers and product managers interested in Bitcoin and related technologies. Bitcoin needs of a diverse set of skills and talents to succeed including Bitcoin protocol devs, Lightning Network app engineers, cryptographers, UX designers and product managers. Assembling residents with a variety of interests will create a more vibrant and dynamic program. Creativity and cross-collaboration will solidify life-long bonds.

The Genesis Center is founded on the principle of recruiting a diverse pool of residents from across the globe. We will support the securing of visas and provide the financial assistance to ensure we are getting the best talent on the planet, which will inspire residents, mentors and hiring partners alike.


Our Volunteer Mentors

Each resident is matched with a mentor who will meet with them for one hour every week for the duration of the program. Mentors are involved from the very beginniner and select their mentees as a criteria for admission.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I get paid to attend the program?
Yes, you do! The intention is not for you to make a profit from the residency, but we do provide a stipend and cover your housing costs (if you are not from the New York area). Many of our residents will be coming from well-paying jobs and may have financial obligations that would otherwise prevent them from participating. The purpose of the stipend is to allow you to commit yourself to the program without taking on debt or otherwise putting yourself in a precarious financial situation.
What kind of people attend the Genesis Center?
The Genesis Center strives to bring together the best and brightest minds and provide them with space and resources to thrive. Talent exists in many different forms and resides all over the world. We believe that by assembling a cohort of residents with differing perspectives will result in a more complete and dynamic experience.
How much programming experience do I need?
The Genesis Center is designed for experienced professionals. We do not believe that years of experience is a predictive measure of mastery, but generally speaking, we consider three years of experience as a minimum requirement.
Is this a bootcamp?
No. This more of a retreat where residents can immerse themselves in their area of interest. Because of the experience level of the residents, we have very little mandated structure and allow curiosity to be the driving force.
What is the time commitment?
Residents are required to come to the Center every work day between the hours of 10 a.m. to 5 p.m. Lunch will be provided every day. Occasional speakers and events will be made available for those interested.
I would like to start my own company. Can I still attend and work on my idea?
Yes. You are welcome to explore your own open source or commercial idea. The Genesis Center will make no claim to your intellectual property or ask for any equity in your commercial endeavor. The Genesis Center’s sole focus is to improve the Bitcoin ecosystem. We ask that you focus your energy on building technology and not business functions like sales, business development, etc. We also prohibit any form of contract work during the program. At the conclusion of the program, we will assist you in every effort to network with investors and assist you in your efforts to build your Bitcoin company.
I am not a US citizen. Can I still attend and will I get visa support?
Yes. We believe that talent resides all over the world and we encourage international students to apply. In some cases, we will be able to sponsor visas.
Will I be forced to take a job after the residency?
No. You are under no obligation to accept a job upon completion of the residency.
What is the admissions process?
Filling out the application is your first step. We will reach out if you have been selected for the second round which will be a more in-depth evaluation of your current skills and how you plan to spend your time at the Genesis Center. Armed with that data, a mentor will select you as a resident they would like to invest in and with work with.
How does the Genesis Center make money?
The Genesis Center has an integrated recruiting agency. We charge companies 25% of a candidate’s first-year salary, excluding bonuses, with a 90-day guarantee. If it doesn’t work out during that time, we’ll cancel the invoice for the full amount. We also depend on the generosity of Bitcoin believers who are willing to invest in making Bitcoin more sustainable.
